## Suggested post body

> Late to the thread but it's a question I keep going back to, and I have one half-useful data point.
>
> Two batches I'm currently rotating differ on exactly this variable:
>
> - **L.A. S.A.G.E. T26 (IPS Pharma, Macedonia)** — gamma-irradiated. Reported THC 26.4%. Limonene-dominant on the nose, with a clear pine-sage backbone.
> - **Karamel Kandy KSV-9 (Kasa Verde, Canada)** — irradiation status "none" on the COA. Reported THC 24.8%. Caryophyllene-dominant with a heavy gas-pepper character.
>
> They're not a controlled experiment — different cultivars, different producers, different harvest dates — but two things stand out subjectively:
>
> 1. The gamma-irradiated batch *smells* less than the non-irradiated one despite having a higher reported total terpene content on the COA. Gamma irradiation does degrade monoterpenes preferentially (limonene, myrcene, pinene), which is what you'd expect to lose first because they're the lighter, more volatile fraction. The β-caryophyllene in KK is heavier and more thermally stable, and it survives.
> 2. Effect-wise, the irradiated batch hits faster and shorter — onset 2–3 minutes, peak around 10–12 — and the non-irradiated one hits slower and longer. Hard to attribute that solely to irradiation given the chemotype differences, but it's consistent with the "monoterpenes degraded so the cannabinoid:terpene synergy is shifted" story.
>
> On the THC inflation question specifically: the relevant mechanism would be **decarboxylation drift**. Gamma irradiation deposits enough energy to push some of the residual THCA → THC, which a post-irradiation HPLC test would then count as THC, not THCA. That's not "inflation" exactly, it's that the test is honest about what's there *now* but the patient is consuming a flower that's already partly decarbed before the vape ever heats it. Same total cannabinoid mass, different ratio at point of use. There's literature on this if anyone wants the specific papers — Hazekamp 2016 on cannabinoid stability, and the more recent Saban et al. 2022 on terpene loss in irradiated flower.
>
> The microseed bit I haven't dug into. My understanding is that gamma irradiation sterilises seeds (so they don't germinate) but doesn't make seeds appear that weren't there. If you're seeing more visible microseeds in irradiated batches, my guess would be selection effect — irradiated flower goes to markets with stricter pathogen testing, which means it's also more likely to be flower that travelled further and was harvested less ideally. But that's a guess; happy to be told I'm wrong.
>
> Two things I'd be curious about, if anyone's got the data:
>
> - Whether anyone's compared **vape temperature** ladders between irradiated and non-irradiated flower of the same cultivar. If the lighter terpenes have already partially evaporated during irradiation, lower temps might give a fairer comparison.
> - Whether the producers themselves have published any post-irradiation COA comparisons. I've not seen any, which is itself telling.
>
> Cheers for kicking this off — it's the right question to be asking and it doesn't get asked enough.
> — Brendon
